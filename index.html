Oct Image Classification

CNV

Choroidal Neovascularization
It's the creation of new blood vessels in the choroid layer of the eye. CNV is often associated with conditions like age-related macular degeneration (AMD) and can cause vision loss due to leakage or bleeding in the macula.

DME

Diabetic Macular Edema
It's a complication of diabetic retinopathy where fluid accumulates in the macula, the central part of the retina. This swelling can cause blurry vision or vision loss in individuals with diabetes.

DRUSEN

Drusen are tiny yellow or white deposits that form under the retina. They are often associated with aging and are a characteristic sign of early-stage age-related macular degeneration (AMD).

NORMAL

In OCT imaging, "normal" refers to the absence of any significant pathological findings or abnormalities in the retina or macula. It indicates that the scanned area of the eye appears healthy without any notable issues.
